ACME CORPORATION
ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE USAGE POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Version 1.0

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy governs the use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools, including large language models (LLMs), generative AI applications, and machine learning systems, by all Acme Corporation employees, contractors, and third-party vendors. It applies to all AI tools used for business purposes, whether company-provided or personally licensed.

2. APPROVED AI TOOLS

The following AI tools are approved for general use across the organization:
- ChatGPT (free and paid versions)
- Microsoft Copilot
- Google Gemini
- Anthropic Claude
- GitHub Copilot
- Any AI tool integrated into existing Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace subscriptions

Employees may use any other publicly available AI tool at their discretion, provided they exercise reasonable judgment.

3. ACCEPTABLE USE

Employees are encouraged to use AI tools to enhance productivity. Permitted uses include:
- Drafting emails, reports, and presentations
- Code generation and debugging
- Summarizing meetings and documents
- Research and analysis
- Customer correspondence
- Translation services
- Data analysis and visualization

4. DATA HANDLING

When using AI tools, employees should use good judgment regarding the information they share. Generally, employees may share:
- Internal documents and communications
- Customer information needed for service delivery
- Source code and technical specifications
- Financial data for analytical purposes
- HR records when seeking AI assistance with personnel matters

Employees do not need to obtain pre-approval before pasting documents into AI tools, as this would slow down productivity.

5. ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT

Employees may sign up for AI services using their corporate email addresses. Personal accounts may also be used for work purposes when more convenient. Password sharing among team members is permitted to reduce subscription costs, provided the password is shared via email or Slack.

6. THIRD-PARTY AI INTEGRATIONS

Department heads have full authority to approve and integrate third-party AI tools and APIs into business workflows without IT review, provided the cost is under $50,000 annually. API keys may be stored in shared team drives for ease of access.

7. AI-GENERATED CONTENT

Content generated by AI may be used in customer-facing communications, marketing materials, legal documents, and contracts without human review when time is of the essence. Employees are trusted to verify accuracy at their discretion.

8. AI IN HIRING AND HR DECISIONS

AI tools may be used to screen resumes, evaluate candidates, and make recommendations regarding promotions, terminations, and compensation. The HR department will periodically review AI recommendations but is not required to document the basis for AI-driven decisions.

9. CODE AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

Employees may paste proprietary source code into public AI tools for debugging or refactoring. AI-generated code may be incorporated into Acme products without additional licensing review. Employees are not required to track which portions of code were AI-generated.

10. CUSTOMER DATA

Customer personally identifiable information (PII), including names, addresses, social security numbers, and payment information, may be shared with AI tools for purposes such as drafting personalized communications or analyzing customer behavior patterns.

11. SECURITY MONITORING

The IT department does not actively monitor AI tool usage, as this is considered an invasion of employee privacy. Employees self-report any incidents involving AI tools.

12. INCIDENT RESPONSE

In the event of a suspected data leak through an AI tool, employees should attempt to delete the conversation history themselves before reporting to IT. Reporting is encouraged but not mandatory.

13. TRAINING AND AWARENESS

Initial AI training is provided during onboarding. No ongoing training or refresher courses are required.

14. POLICY EXCEPTIONS

Any employee may request an exception to this policy by sending an email to their direct manager. Exceptions are typically granted within 24 hours.

15. ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this policy will be addressed on a case-by-case basis. There are no specified penalties for violations.

16. POLICY REVIEW

This policy will be reviewed every five years or as needed.
